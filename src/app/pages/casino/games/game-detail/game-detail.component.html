BACCARAT

Cards are dealt to the Player and Banker, and the winner is the side that is nearest to 9

GUIDELINES

Game Table

  • The player places his bet on the layout, which includes the following areas: Player, Banker, Tie, and Pair.
  • The dealer deals two cards each to the Player and the Banker.
  • The dealer opens the Player’s cards and then the Banker’s. A third card may or may not be dealt to either the Player or the Banker depending on the baccarat rules.
  • The side with the hand closest to 9 wins, and the winner receives the amount of his original bet.(1 to 1)

GAME RULES

Value of Card

  • Aces are valued at 1.
  • Kings, Queens, Jacks, and 10s are valued at 0.
  • Cards from 2 to 9 are taken at face value.
  • If the total of the cards exceeds 10, only the second digit is counted.
The winner receives the amount of the original bet.(1 to 1)

[If the Banker wins, a 5% commission is deducted]※ In no commission table, there is no deduction. However, if the banker wins with total value of 6, 50% commission will be paid to the house.

If a tie bet is won, the player receives eight times the original bet.(8 to 1)

※ Tie : the total of the Player’s and Banker’s cards are equal

If a pair bet is won, the player receives eleven times the original bet. (11 to 1)

※ Pair : the first two cards are the same value

BLACKJACK

Blackjack is one of the most popular card games in casino, lt also known as"Twenty-one." The person with the hand closest to 21 wins

GUIDELINES

Game Table

Hand Combination

  • After betting, all players and the dealer receive two cards.
  • The dealer places one of his cards face-up.
  • The dealer must take cards until his hand reaches 17.
  • A player may receive additional cards (“hit”) as long as his hand does not exceed 21, or he may stay with his given hand.

GAME RULES

Value of Card

  • Aces are worth either 1 or 11, whichever is preferable
  • King, Queen, Jack are worth 10 respectively
  • Other cards are represented by their number on the card.
Blackjack

Aces are worth either 1 or 11, whichever is preferable

Bust

When a player’s first two cards total 21, he is paid 1.5 times the amount of the bet. (1.5 to 1)

Push

If a player’s and dealer’s hands are equal, a tie results.

Stay

Wave your hand from left to right A player does not want any additional cards. If the dealer’s hand exceeds 17, he cannot take any further cards.

Hit

Tap the table with a finger
Tap the table with a finger. A player asks the dealer for another card in addition to his first two cards

Pair Bet

If a player anticipates that the first two cards are pair cards, he can place a pair bet. If he gets pair cards, he will be paid 11 times amount of the bet..

Surrender

When a player thinks that his first two cards cannot beat the dealer’s cards, he may surrender the game and forfeit half of his initial bet.

Insurance

When the dealer’s face-up card is an ace, players can post insurance of up to half the initial bet.
- If the dealer has a blackjack, insurance bet pays 2 to 1.
- If the dealer doesn’t have a blackjack, insurance bet is lost.

Even Money

If a player’s first two cards are blackjack and the dealer’s face-up card is an ace, the player may choose even money. In this case, the player will be paid for the original bet.
(If the player does not choose even money and the dealer’s hand is blackjack, it will be a push)

Double Down

If a player requests, he may place an additional bet not exceeding the current bet, and receive only one additional card.

Split

If a player’s first two cards are of the equal value, he may split them into two hands and bet on each hand separately. The amount of the bet must equal the initial bet.
